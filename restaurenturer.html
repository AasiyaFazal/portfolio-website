    Project Overview

    For this project, the task was to create a location-based recommendation app. Being a self professed foodie, the best part of visiting any place in the world is visiting restaurants with unique, authentic food and a beautifully memorable ambience. This app aims to make it effortless and seamless for users to find unique food and drink experiences wherever they are.

    Competitor Analysis

    Competitor apps were identified as any food and drink recommendation apps. The two apps chosen for my competitor and UX analysis were: Foursquare and OpenTable.

    Insights gained from analysing these apps were key to generating the goals, objectives and hypotheses to inform the user research stage of the design process. In addition, weaknesses identified in the user experience of these apps helped to identify areas my app could be designed differently to provide an improved experience.

    Foursquare

    This app helps users find the best places to eat, drink, shop, or visit in any city in the world.

    This is the first screen a user sees when they go onto the app. It allows a user to start searching for places straight away. However, a weakness is that there are no suggested places to browse through before a user searches as on other apps such as OpenTable, UberEats, Deliveroo.

    This screen layout is easy to understand and the information given to users is relevant i.e. ratings, distance. However, the design is not as aesthetically pleasing as other apps. A unique feature over other apps is the short tips by users and local experts. This feature was incorporated into my app design.

    This is the screen a user sees when they select a place to view more information about. The tabs which provide different sets of key information about a restaurant and button to add a place to a list were included in my design. My design included the latter on the restaurant search page too.

    OpenTable

    This app enables users to book and enjoy extraordinary dining experiences worldwide based on their personal preferences.

    This is the first screen a user sees when they go onto the desktop version of the app. It allows a user to search for restaurants or browse suggested restaurants straight away without logging in. The design is clear, intuitive and aesthetically pleasing. It inspired the design of the desktop screens for my app.

    This is the first screen a user sees when they go onto the mobile app. They can browse through restaurants, see which have tables available and save restaurants. These features were incorporated into my design.

    The tabs on this screen which separate different types of relevant information about a restaurant were incorporated into my design. The icons which allow a user to save and share a restaurant were also included.

    2. User Research

    The competitor and UX analysis above helped to shape the hypotheses and objectives for user research conducted as part of the discovery stage. 4 user interviews were conducted as part of this and key insights highlighted are shown within this section.

    Insight 1

    All participants wanted to be able to search for restaurants based on location, rating, ambience, cuisine and dietary requirements.

    Insight 1

    All participants wanted to be able to search for restaurants based on location, rating, ambience, cuisine and dietary requirements.

    Insight 2

    All participants felt it was important to have a restaurant’s waiting times at different day-times when deciding to go to a restaurant which does not allow table reservations.

    Insight 2

    All participants felt it was important to have a restaurant’s waiting times at different day-times when deciding to go to a restaurant which does not allow table reservations.

    Insight 3

    3 of 4 participants value restaurant recommendations from local experts and food bloggers.

    Insight 3

    3 of 4 participants value restaurant recommendations from local experts and food bloggers.

    Insight 4

    3 of 4 participants share potential restaurants they want to go to with others via WhatsApp.

    Insight 4

    3 of 4 participants share potential restaurants they want to go to with others via WhatsApp.

    Insight 5

    3 of 4 participants prefer booking a table online when they find a restaurant rather than calling up the restaurant.
