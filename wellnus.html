    • WEllnUS

    Project Overview

    Maintaining a balanced life is becoming increasingly more important for your personal health and wellbeing in a fast paced, modern world. WEllnUS is an app which aims for anybody and everybody to feel empowered and not alone in achieving their health and wellbeing goals through:

    1. Making it easier to plan and cook healthy, balanced meals at home.
    2. Helping to connect individuals with similar life circumstances and goals so they can exchange helpful tips and support.

    Background and Discovery

    During an internship at a Health Technology company, I organised and facilitated user discovery sessions with a diversity of individuals who either had Diabetes or Obesity.

    The following themes were highlighted, in particular, for ethnic minority mothers:

    1. It was difficult achieving their health and wellbeing goals due to the daily struggle of having to prepare a separate dinner for themselves and their families.
    2. They felt alone in their journey. There was a desire to have support from someone outside of their family who had similar goals and life circumstances.

    Competitor Analysis

    1. Meal Plan Apps

    At the time of the competitor analysis, there were no apps on the market which allowed group meal planning. Therefore an analysis of individual meal planning apps was carried out.

    Mealtime

    This app’s format for displaying in app recipes inspired the design of the search in app recipes page of my app.

    MealPrepPro

    This app’s general format for displaying a weekly meal plan was the most intuitive from meal plan apps researched and inspired the layout of my design.

    2. Social Apps

    Whilst plenty of social apps exist for finding friends and partners such as Facebook, LinkedIn and Bumble, during the competitive analysis, it was found that there are no social apps specifically aimed at helping someone find a support buddy with similar life circumstances who can support and encourage them as they progress towards achieving their general health and wellbeing goals.

    Peanut

    This app connects women who are at a similar stage in life. This was the only social app found to be targeted at helping individuals find support from people with similar life circumstances.

    We3

    This app’s format for displaying commonalities between a user and a suggested friend inspired the design of my screen for displaying commonalities between a user and a suggested support buddy.

    Testing

    1. Create Meal Plan

    The main insights gained from user testing of Create Meal Plan v1 to create the improved v2 screen (both shown below) were as follows:

    • The screen didn’t give users the flexibility of picking different meals to plan for each day; for instance, on Monday, a user might want to plan breakfast and dinner but on Wednesday, Lunch and Dinner instead.
    • It wasn’t clear to the users what would happen when the Add New Meal button was selected.

    Create Meal Plan v1

    Create Meal Plan v2

    2. My Network

    The main insights gained from user testing of My Network v1 to create the improved v2 screen (both shown below) were as follows:

    • The screen did not incorporate family and friends.
    • Instead of a button to view all support buddies, it would be more intuitive to have a scroll across panel with different categories i.e. support buddies, family etc.
    • It would be a better user experience if there was an icon to go to messages rather than having all chats display on the My Network page.
